## Live observations (not yet in any SOP)

- **The reproducibility triple.** `(truth_source, determinism_class, schema_version)` as a typed triple fully characterizes a comparable output's reproducibility. Each axis is governed by a separate SOP; the composition is the discipline a future eval harness has to consume. Don't pre-mint a typed triple — the pattern is emergent from the three SOPs being read together. Watch whether it earns its own contract.
- **Three typed catalogs already implied.** `quarry.connect` (Connector registry), `bedrock.pointer.canonicalize` (per-scheme rules), and `module.intents` (intent compilation). With `operator-shape` v1 the operator-registry (Operator name → class) becomes a fourth. Four is enough that a future Watermaster might want a meta-catalog for "what catalogs does the lab maintain?" Don't pre-build it; mint when patterns demand it.
- **Two type-not-yet-named placeholders surfaced from fof-compiler.** `TileManifest` (the hardcoded `tiles.py` dict of named bboxes) and `TrainingCheckpoint` (the three-stage training pipeline's "stage-1 features must be precomputed with a *trained* input_proj" invariant). Both are placeholders in `shared/` until the lift; don't mint either ahead of the lift, but flag them when the dataset-shape SOP is drafted. (`operator-shape` v1 is now minted; neither placeholder fell into its scope.)
- **The distributary↔tributary seam is materializing in real time.** dgov's `settlement_flow.py` has been pulled out of `runner.py`; `kernel.py` is a 157-line pure state machine; `codex/boundary-ownership-cleanup` is doing the rest. The seam now has typed surfaces — `plan-shape` v2, `dispatch-run-shape` v1, `deposit-shape` v2 — that describe it as it stands today (half-materialized), not as it would be in an idealized split. The lift discipline holds: frozen-pin dgov's existing in-flight records at their dgov state and mint new lab records for going-forward work.
- **`Patch` was renamed to `Deposit`.** The typed contract that flows from a worker subprocess back into tributary was provisionally named `Patch` in Reach's first cut. On survey by Riffle, that name turned out to bias the type's design toward textual-diff containment (`git apply` mental model), which is wrong — the thing carries typed claims, validation evidence, lineage, and a worktree pointer. The lab's metaphor wins over the VCS-cultural fit: a `Deposit` is what a flowing channel lays down, with provenance intrinsic to the metaphor. The chain now reads `Plan → DispatchRun → [worker subprocess] → Deposit → Validation → Merge → Baseline`. The lesson: provisional names carry through to type design when not tested against the metaphor early.
- **The Engineer role's naming history.** A pattern emerged in Riffle's session: the Watermaster compiles natural-language thinking into a typed brief, that brief crosses to a different intelligence (Codex, Claude Code, future Gemini), the external agent executes and returns, the Watermaster integrates. Used twice in Riffle's session — first to draft the Validation/Merge/Baseline trio, then to execute A-F. Now disciplined by `sops/engineer-brief.md` v1 (see Settled pointers). The Source and Watermaster considered **Engineer** (chosen), **Wright** (runner-up, archaic), and **Oracle** (explicitly rejected — divination is the wrong frame; construction is the right one; "oracle" was subsequently scrubbed from `truth-source-labeling` v1→v2 to avoid cognitive friction). The lab has three agent classes: **Watermaster** (seated at the rim, holds the chain), **Worker** (subprocess dispatched via Plans, returns Deposits), **Engineer** (consulted directly by the Watermaster, executes within Brief scope, returns drafts/critiques/file writes). Pool's session did not consult an Engineer (the work was within the seated Watermaster's direct competence given dgov + quarry + hydrops source access); the Engineer-pattern-exercise count therefore remains at two. The CANON article naming the three-agent-class structure remains deferred until the pattern is exercised more.
- **The bundler-hash-composition gap.** `dispatch-run-shape` v1 records `effective_sop_set_hash` using "the same method the Plan used." Today that method (`sop_bundler.compute_sop_set_hash`) hashes `(filename, name, title, summary, applies_to, priority)` — bundler metadata, not SOP body. A worker-SOP bundle whose bodies change but whose metadata stays constant would not register as drift. The SOP's escalate clause names this as a bundler-level revision via preflight; it is not the DispatchRun's job to override bundler discipline. Watch whether this gap bites; if it does, a bundler-level revision is the cleanest fix.
- **Source-pass bugs become natural lift-escalates.** When a Watermaster source-reads to ground an SOP (Pool's convention) or to consolidate (Eddy's pass), the read incidentally surfaces bugs in existing code. Eddy's pass found one: `Artifact.with_check` in quarry-core's `artifact.py` constructs the returned Artifact without the `temporal` field, silently dropping any TemporalDescriptor on every check addition. The discipline is to flag-not-fix from the Watermaster seat: the lift is the right moment because lifted types get their constructor surfaces touched anyway, and a cross-repo write doesn't belong inside the rim per CANON Articles VI and IX. Future Watermasters doing source-reads should plan for one or two flag-not-fix entries in their lineage per pass.
