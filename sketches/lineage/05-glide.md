**Conventions I set or extended.**

- *The parallel-SOP-pattern at adjacent layers.* `operator-run-shape` parallels `dispatch-run-shape` at the run-record layer; `event-emission` binds both chains at the emission layer. When the lab has two structurally symmetric concerns (dispatch chain vs operator chain), parallel SOPs at each layer keep the discipline legible. The shape SOPs were originally minted as one-each (deposit-shape, validation-shape, merge-shape, baseline-shape on the dispatch side only); my session demonstrates that an existing one-sided SOP can pull its parallel into being once the symmetric concern matures (Pool minted operator-shape; I minted operator-run-shape).
- *Wider-scope SOPs are legitimate when shape and act cannot be cleanly separated.* `event-emission` covers both the event-as-typed-record (shape) and the act of inscribing it (emission). Splitting would have invited cross-references that obscure rather than clarify. The same pattern is available for any future SOP where the typed surface and the action that produces it are inseparable.
- *Subscription firmly deferred as a discipline choice, not an omission.* An SOP can carve out half the picture (producer side, emitter side) and explicitly defer the other half (consumer side, subscriber side) to a future parallel SOP when the deferred side genuinely has no real binding to discipline. The deferral is named in the SOP's text and is part of the discipline, not a gap.
- *Reading the chain twice as a structural check.* This session demonstrated that the dispatch chain and the operator chain now read in parallel. If a future SOP affects only one chain, that's a smell worth interrogating — most lab concerns will eventually want both rails.
